Strategic Prospects for Black Sea Shipping After the War

Introduction: A Region at a Turning Point

The Black Sea has always been a vital trade bridge between Europe, the Caucasus, Central Asia and the Middle East. The ongoing war dramatically reduced cargo turnover, but even in 2025 Ukraine has already exported over 25 million tons of grain through its temporary corridor — exceeding pre-war levels. Once peace returns, the region is set to become one of the fastest-growing maritime corridors in the world.

Post-War Scenarios & Outlook

Three realistic outcomes will shape the future:

  • Ceasefire with current lines — fastest recovery (2–3 years), $5–10 bn infrastructure investments from EU/US.
  • Full Ukrainian control of coastline — Odessa becomes a 50 mtpa grain hub, integration into EU Green Lanes and Green Deal.
  • Frozen conflict — traffic shifts to Romania, Bulgaria, Georgia and Turkey (Middle Corridor boom).

Atlantic Council and PortEconomics forecast return to pre-war volumes by 2028 and +20–30% growth by 2035.

Infrastructure & Trade Boom Ahead

Expected developments:

  • Odessa & Chornomorsk deep-water upgrades (18 m draft for Panamax+ vessels)
  • Constanta (Romania) and Poti/Batumi (Georgia) +25–40% capacity increase
  • TRACECA and Middle Corridor rail-ferry integration with China–EU traffic
  • LNG and green ammonia bunkering hubs
